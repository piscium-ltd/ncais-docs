# Nairobi County Assembly Integrated System (NCAIS)

Welcome to the official documentation for **NCAIS** – the **Nairobi County Assembly Integrated System**.  
This system integrates multiple departments and processes to streamline the operations of the County Assembly.

---

### Modules Overview

The system is organized into the following **modules**, each containing its own set of processes:

#### Legal

- Legislative
- Committee Services
- Legal Audit
- Litigation Management
- Legal Writing & Advisory
- Conveyancing Services
- Contract Drafting

#### Administration

- Asset Management
- Transport Management
- Hospitality
- Cleaning Process
- Laundry Services

#### Human Resource

- Workforce Planning and Employment
- Employee Lifecycle Management
- Performance, Learning and Growth
- Employee Insurance
- Employee Relations & Welfare
- Digital HR & Analytics

#### Internal Audit

- Internal Audit Configuration and Methodology Libraries
- Evidence Management & Chain of Custody
- Audit Findings, Reporting & Compliance Oversight
- Audit Committee Governance & Quality Assurance
- Management Action & Follow-Up on Audit Recommendations

#### Finance

- Budgeting & Appropriation
- Integration & Reconciliation
- Exchequer & Cash-Flow Management
- Gratuity
- Financial Reporting & Analytics
- Scheme Funds – Car Loan & Mortgage

#### Supply Chain Management

- Stores Management
- Procurement Planning
- Supplier Registration
- Contract Management
- File Tracking
- Records Management

#### Accounts

- Accounts Payable
- Fixed Assets
- Imprest Management

---

## Getting Started

1. Navigate to any module in the sidebar to explore its processes.
2. Each process page contains step-by-step instructions, reference materials, and workflow guidelines.
3. Use the search bar (top right) to quickly find any process or guideline.

---

## Contributing

If you are contributing to NCAIS documentation:

- Follow the folder structure in `docs/`.
- Create a markdown file for new processes under the appropriate module folder.
- Update `mkdocs.yml` navigation if adding a new page.
- Follow existing style conventions for headings and lists.
